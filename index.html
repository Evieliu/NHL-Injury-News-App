How much is your hockey team injured?

According to a team of researchers at Toronto's St. Michael's Hospital, injuries sustained by players in the NHL cost the league nearly $218 million each season. The cost of concussions alone was an astonishing $42.8 million a season.

Team insurance covers some of the losses. Policies generally cover 80 percent of lost salary if a player loses more than 30 games to a single injury. But the high cost of insurance and the very high cost of uninsured injury is absorbed by the various NHL teams â€” and then passed on to ticket buyers. This raised the questions: How are NHL teams impacted by injuries?

Are there some teams that suffered more, some less? Are there more vulnerable positions on the hockey field than others? Do the top-value players get injured most, or the opposite? How many dollors did NHL teams lose for injuries? Does that account for a big proportion of their financial statements?

And more deeply, how would the game result be different if we exclude the injury influence? If a team could have performed much much better without injuries, does that mean it needs to change some of its strategies?

In this data journalism project, all the injury data of 2013-14 NHL regular season are analyzed to see how they have impacted the league's performance and financial status. You can either search by player name or select a team below to start exploring the numbers. To customize your intereted points, such as comparison between specific teams and positions, ranking within divisions, filtering by different parameters, check the interactive Tableau chart at the bottom of the page.
